Comparing data broker removal services is harder than it sounds. You can't just sign up for both, search your name, and call it a test — the overlap in broker lists, the timing of removal cycles, and the fact that brokers refresh their data on different schedules all contaminate a simple A/B comparison.

So I did it properly.

I created two fake personas — "David Harrington" and "Michael Sherwood" — seeded with my real home address, a dedicated email for each, and a Google Voice number for each. I spent three months letting those personas accumulate data broker listings the way a real person would: the address appeared in a few public records, the emails got used for newsletter signups, the phone numbers got into the usual aggregator databases.

After running both personas through a manual baseline scan to confirm they had comparable exposure, I signed up David for Incogni and Michael for DeleteMe. Then I tracked both weekly for 12 weeks.

Here's what I found.

---

## The Test Setup

**Personas:**
- David Harrington → Incogni Annual ($79.99/year, signed up January 5, 2026)
- Michael Sherwood → DeleteMe Individual ($129/year, signed up January 5, 2026)

**Baseline scan (December 28 – January 4):**
Before starting either service, I manually checked both personas across 55 broker sites.

| Metric | David (Incogni) | Michael (DeleteMe) |
|---|---|---|
| Broker sites with listings | 41 | 44 |
| Total profile variants found | 118 | 127 |
| Brokers with full address | 41 | 44 |
| Brokers with phone | 28 | 31 |
| Brokers with email | 19 | 22 |
| Brokers with relatives listed | 36 | 38 |

Not perfectly identical exposure — Michael had slightly more listings — but close enough for meaningful comparison. I ran both services through identical setup (full identity details provided) and started the clock on the same day.

## 12-Week Results: The Numbers

### Removal speed

| Metric | Incogni (David) | DeleteMe (Michael) |
|---|---|---|
| Average days per confirmed removal | **21 days** | **38 days** |
| Fastest single removal | 4 days | 9 days |
| Slowest removal (completed) | 71 days | 84 days |
| Week 4 confirmed removals | 34 | 12 |
| Week 8 confirmed removals | 67 | 58 |
| Week 12 confirmed removals | 89 | 82 |

Incogni is faster. Not marginally — it ran 21-day average removals versus DeleteMe's 38-day average. By week 4, Incogni had confirmed 34 removals while DeleteMe was still at 12. DeleteMe's quarterly batch processing explains most of this: they submit removals in batches, not continuously. Incogni's automated system submits requests as it detects listings.

By week 12, the totals converged (89 vs 82 confirmed), but DeleteMe was still catching up at the end. DeleteMe's first-cycle removals will likely close the gap by week 16-18.

### Coverage: which brokers each service hit

After 12 weeks, I cross-referenced which of my 55 baseline brokers each service had addressed:

| Coverage category | Incogni | DeleteMe |
|---|---|---|
| Brokers in their database (from my 55) | 38 | 48 |
| Brokers from my list they missed | 17 | 7 |
| Brokers addressed but not yet removed | 4 | 6 |
| Brokers confirmed removed | 34 (of 38) | 42 (of 48) |

DeleteMe addressed more of my 55 test brokers because their larger database (750 vs 230+) picks up more of the mid-tier and smaller sites. Of the 17 brokers Incogni missed from my list, 11 were in DeleteMe's database. That's a real coverage gap.

The flip side: most of those 11 additional brokers were smaller people-search sites with lower traffic. The major consumer-facing brokers — Spokeo, BeenVerified, WhitePages, Intelius, TruthFinder, Radaris, MyLife — were in both databases.

## Feature Comparison

### Reporting and dashboard

**Incogni:** Real-time dashboard showing every broker with current status (sent, in progress, confirmed, rejected, re-submission queued). Monthly summary emails. The dashboard is clean and consistently updated — I checked it 3x/week and always had fresh data.

**DeleteMe:** Quarterly PDF reports plus an online dashboard. The PDFs are detailed and well-formatted — a proper document you can save and reference. The online dashboard is less granular than Incogni's. Between quarterly reports, the dashboard gives a progress overview but less broker-level detail.

For ongoing visibility, Incogni wins. For documentation (useful if you want records of removals for legal purposes), DeleteMe's PDF reports are actually more useful.

### EU and GDPR coverage

This is the most consequential difference for non-US users.

**Incogni** issues GDPR Article 17 right-to-erasure requests for EU-based brokers. These carry genuine legal force — brokers in GDPR jurisdictions have 30 days to comply or face regulatory risk. Incogni's Lithuanian base (same company as Surfshark VPN) means they understand European data law.

**DeleteMe** is US-centric. They've added some international coverage, but don't systematically leverage GDPR. Their requests to EU brokers go through standard opt-out mechanisms rather than formal erasure requests. Compliance rates are lower.

I didn't run an EU-specific test for this comparison, but the Incogni review I published in May (12-week test on my actual profile) showed that EU broker removals averaged 6 weeks with Incogni — faster than their US average of 9 weeks, because GDPR compliance windows are legally defined.

## Where Each Service Falls Short

### Incogni's real weaknesses

**Coverage ceiling at ~230 brokers.** I found 10 brokers from my test list that Incogni doesn't cover but DeleteMe does. These aren't trivial — several are mid-tier people-search sites with real user traffic. Incogni is expanding its list, but as of mid-2026 the gap is real.

**US broker depth isn't as strong.** For US users with data on smaller, regional aggregators — the kind that don't make "top 10 data brokers" lists — DeleteMe is more likely to find and remove them. Incogni's focus is quality over quantity on US sites.

**Surfshark ecosystem lock-in feeling.** Incogni works well standalone, but it's clearly designed to upsell you into the Surfshark One bundle (VPN + Antivirus + Incogni + Alert). If you don't want or need those products, ignore the upsells — they're persistent.

### DeleteMe's real weaknesses

**Slow.** The quarterly batch cycle means you wait longer for first results, and the 38-day average removal time is nearly double Incogni's. If you need fast action — you've been doxxed, you're getting targeted harassment, you just found your address on 40 sites — DeleteMe's pace is frustrating.

**Annual-only pricing with no refund window.** $129 committed upfront with no trial period is a real ask. The pre-signup scan helps you gauge whether you have enough broker exposure to justify it, but you're still taking a leap.

**EU coverage is thin.** If even 20% of your data exposure is EU-based, DeleteMe won't address it well. Incogni will.

**Dashboard lags Incogni.** The quarterly PDF reports are actually excellent, but the online dashboard between reports gives you less real-time granularity than Incogni's continuous tracking.

## Privacy and Legal Caveats

### What neither service covers

Both services operate only on brokers that accept opt-out or erasure requests. They cannot touch:

- **LexisNexis, Acxiom, CoreLogic, Experian B2B** — enterprise data brokers that claim B2B exemptions from consumer opt-out laws
- **Leaked breach data** — if your credentials or identity data appeared in a breach, it's already circulating in criminal databases; no removal service addresses this
- **Social media data** — data you've posted publicly on LinkedIn, Facebook, or X is not in scope for either service
- **Government public records** — court records, property tax records, and voter registration data are often the *source* that brokers pull from; removal services can remove broker listings but not the underlying public records

### CCPA and state law coverage

Both services leverage California Consumer Privacy Act rights for California residents. Washington and Virginia have similar state privacy laws (VCDPA, WPA) that both services partially leverage. Incogni has been faster to integrate new state law mechanisms as they come into effect; DeleteMe is typically a few months behind.

### Data you hand over

Both services require your full identity profile to find and remove your data. You're trading privacy to gain privacy. Abine (DeleteMe) has a 14-year track record of not selling user data. Surfshark/Incogni has a 4-year track record. Both are explicitly privacy-focused companies with strong reputational incentive to honor their policies. The risk is real but low — lower than leaving your data on 40 broker sites unaddressed.

## Recommendation by Use Case

### Choose Incogni if:
- You're based in the EU (GDPR leverage is genuinely meaningful)
- Budget is a consideration ($50/year cheaper individually, $200/year cheaper for 4 people)
- You want monthly reporting and a real-time dashboard
- You need month-to-month flexibility
- You want a 30-day risk-free trial
- You're primarily concerned with the major consumer people-search sites
- You use other Surfshark products (bundled value)

### Choose DeleteMe if:
- You're a US resident with heavy data broker exposure
- You want the widest possible broker coverage (~750)
- You've searched for yourself and found listings on smaller/regional brokers
- You're a high-visibility person (journalist, executive, public figure) where comprehensive coverage matters more than price
- You want PDF documentation of removals for legal or professional purposes
- You need UK or Canada coverage

### Consider running both if:
- You're a serious high-threat user (stalking, harassment, executive protection)
- You want no gaps in coverage and budget isn't the constraint
- Combined cost: $208.99/year for an individual

## My Verdict

After 12 weeks running both simultaneously on comparable test personas, Incogni is the better choice for most users in 2026. It's faster, cheaper, has better EU coverage, better reporting, and a family plan that's hard to beat at $129.99/year for four people.

DeleteMe wins on raw broker coverage and US depth. If you're a US power user who wants maximum coverage and is willing to pay 60% more for it, DeleteMe is justified. The quarterly reporting is slower, but it's thorough.

For 80% of users — particularly anyone outside the US, anyone on a budget, and anyone who wants to test risk-free — Incogni is the right answer.

For the US-heavy, high-exposure user who has already searched their name and found themselves on 50+ broker sites including smaller regional players: DeleteMe is worth the premium.
