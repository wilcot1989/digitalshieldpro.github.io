## The Data Broker Industry: Context That Matters

Data brokers are largely unregulated in the United States at the federal level. While California's CCPA and other state laws give residents the right to request deletion of their data, most states have no such protections. This is why removal services have to work on your behalf using the legal tools that exist — and why the removals are never permanent.

The industry is enormous: Acxiom, one of the largest data brokers, reportedly holds data on 2.5 billion people. LexisNexis, Equifax, and TransUnion all have data broker divisions. Thousands of smaller players resell slices of their databases. Your personal information is a commodity that has been bought and sold many times without your knowledge or consent.

Data broker removal services like Optery and Incogni are not a perfect solution to this — they are a practical mitigation. They reduce your exposure, make it harder for people to find your personal information quickly, and force brokers to spend resources processing your requests. That has real value even if it is not a permanent fix.
