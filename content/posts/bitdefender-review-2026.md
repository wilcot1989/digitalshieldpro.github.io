## Common Mistakes When Choosing Antivirus Software

After 10 years in cybersecurity, I keep seeing the same errors. Don't be that person.

### 1. Picking based on first-year price alone

Bitdefender's $19.99 first year is incredible. But at renewal you're paying $89.99. Norton jumps from $49.99 to $104.99. If you're budget-sensitive, calculate the 3-year cost, not the Year 1 sticker price. Or do what I do: set a reminder to cancel before renewal and re-subscribe at a promotional rate.

### 2. Keeping Windows Defender and thinking you're protected

Windows Defender is decent for basic threats. But in my testing it consistently misses fileless malware, advanced phishing, and some zero-day samples that Bitdefender catches. Defender also lacks ransomware remediation, a firewall worth configuring, and parental controls. "Free" isn't always cheap when a ransomware attack costs you every photo you've ever taken.

### 3. Paying extra for features you already have

If you already use NordVPN, you don't need Bitdefender's Premium VPN. If you use Bitwarden, skip the password manager. Total Security at $19.99 gives you the core protection engine. Don't upgrade to Premium Security at $79.99 for a VPN that isn't as good as your standalone one.

### 4. Ignoring the renewal email and getting auto-charged

Every antivirus company does this. They bury the renewal date, enable auto-renew by default, and charge your card at the full rate. The moment you purchase Bitdefender, open your calendar and create a reminder 11 months out. Check for promotional deals, and if none are available, cancel and re-purchase through a fresh promotional link.

### 5. Not checking platform coverage before buying

Antivirus Plus is Windows-only. If you have a Mac, Android phone, and Windows laptop, you need Total Security minimum. I've talked to people who bought the cheapest plan and then realized they couldn't install it on half their devices.
