## What You Actually Pay More For

### 1. Server Infrastructure: Owned vs. Rented

This is the single biggest infrastructure differentiator that affects both security and performance.

**Rented servers** (virtual private servers from third-party data centers) are cheaper and faster to deploy globally. Most VPN providers use a mix of rented and owned infrastructure. The risk with rented servers is that the data center operator could theoretically access server hardware — which matters if you have a no-logs policy that might otherwise be credible.

**Owned servers** (co-located or dedicated hardware fully under the VPN provider's control) cost significantly more but provide stronger assurances. NordVPN owns its server network and physically controls access. ExpressVPN runs Lightway protocol on a mix of owned and managed infrastructure. Mullvad co-locates its own hardware in data centers.

**RAM-only servers** represent an additional premium: servers that run entirely in RAM, with no persistent storage, so that a physical seizure of server hardware yields nothing useful. NordVPN, ExpressVPN, and Proton VPN all use RAM-only infrastructure.

### 2. Independent Security Audits

Audits cost real money — typically $50,000 to $200,000+ per engagement — and responsible providers pay for them regularly.

**What audit-heavy providers spend on:**
- No-logs policy verification by independent firms (Cure53, Deloitte, PwC)
- Application security audits across all platforms
- Browser extension audits
- Network configuration audits
- Server infrastructure audits

**Audit track records:**
- **NordVPN:** Annual no-logs audits (Deloitte), multiple infrastructure and app audits. Survived a 2018 server breach with no user data exposed — the most meaningful real-world test of a no-logs policy.
- **ExpressVPN:** Multiple Cure53 audits, RAM-disk infrastructure audit. Passed a real-world test when a server was seized in the Andel murder investigation — authorities found no usable data.
- **Surfshark:** Regular Cure53 audits, ISO 27001 certified.
- **Proton VPN:** Open-source clients, independent audits, Swiss privacy law backing.

**CyberGhost** conducts quarterly transparency reports (which is unusual and valuable) but has had fewer deep infrastructure audits than the top tier providers.

### 3. Protocol Development and Innovation

The underlying protocol — the technology that actually creates and secures your VPN tunnel — matters significantly for speed and security.

**OpenVPN** is the industry standard: open-source, battle-tested, and widely audited. Almost every provider offers it.

**WireGuard** is the modern alternative: significantly faster than OpenVPN, with a much smaller codebase (fewer lines of code = fewer potential vulnerabilities). Most major providers now support WireGuard.

**Proprietary protocols** represent real R&D investment:
- **Lightway** (ExpressVPN): Developed in-house, open-sourced and audited, genuinely fast. This is partly why ExpressVPN commands a premium.
- **NordLynx** (NordVPN): WireGuard implementation with an additional privacy layer to solve WireGuard's IP logging issue. Excellent performance.
- **Hydra** (Hotspot Shield/Aura): Proprietary but less transparent.

When you pay more for ExpressVPN, you are partly paying for Lightway protocol development. Whether that is worth the premium depends on whether you actually notice the performance difference — which, in my testing, is often minimal compared to NordLynx.

### 4. Server Count and Geographic Coverage

More servers generally means better performance and more location options. But raw server count can be misleading.

**Quality over quantity:** A provider with 5,000 well-maintained, owned, RAM-only servers is preferable to one with 9,000 virtual servers on rented infrastructure with unclear ownership.

**Virtual server locations:** Some providers advertise a server location (say, Brazil) but actually route traffic through a server physically located elsewhere. This is not always disclosed. For most users it doesn't matter; for journalists and activists in specific countries, it can.

**Specialty servers:** Some use cases require specific server types:
- **Obfuscated servers**: Make VPN traffic look like regular HTTPS traffic, bypassing VPN blocks in China, UAE, Russia. NordVPN and ExpressVPN both offer these.
- **Double VPN / Multi-hop**: Routes traffic through two VPN servers for extra layers. NordVPN, ProtonVPN, and Mullvad offer this.
- **Tor over VPN**: Routes traffic through the Tor network after the VPN. NordVPN's Onion over VPN servers offer this.
- **Static IP / Dedicated IP**: Gives you a fixed IP address. Useful for businesses that whitelist IPs. NordVPN and PIA offer this as a paid add-on.

If you need obfuscated servers or multi-hop, you need a provider that invests in those features — which tends to push you toward mid-range pricing.

### 5. Customer Support Quality

This sounds soft but actually represents real cost. 24/7 live chat support with knowledgeable staff costs money. The budget providers with excellent support (Surfshark, notably) are doing so as a competitive differentiator, accepting lower margins on the service.

ExpressVPN and NordVPN both offer strong live chat support. Mullvad's support is email-only, which reflects their privacy-first model (no accounts means no account-linked support tickets) rather than cost-cutting.

## Where You Are Paying for Marketing, Not Infrastructure

### The ExpressVPN Premium

ExpressVPN is genuinely good, but it is also genuinely overpriced relative to NordVPN and Surfshark for most users. In my testing:
- Speed: Comparable to NordVPN using NordLynx. Sometimes faster, sometimes slower. No consistent advantage.
- Privacy: Both have independently audited no-logs policies and RAM-only servers. Effectively equivalent.
- Features: ExpressVPN has slightly fewer specialty servers (no multi-hop, no Tor-over-VPN). NordVPN wins on features.
- Apps: ExpressVPN's apps are excellent. NordVPN's are also excellent. Both score similarly.

ExpressVPN was the first major VPN to build strong brand recognition, and that brand commands a premium. If you have used it for years and trust it, there is no urgent reason to switch. But if you are starting fresh, NordVPN at $3.69/month versus ExpressVPN at $6.67/month is a significant price difference for effectively equivalent security.

### Overpaying on Monthly Plans

The monthly plan price for most VPNs ranges from $11 to $15. This is approximately 3-4x the effective price of a 2-year subscription for the same service. The only reason to pay monthly is if you genuinely only need the VPN for a short period. For ongoing use, the 2-year plan is almost always the correct economic choice.

**One important caveat:** If you are trying a new provider, pay monthly first for one or two months before committing to a 2-year subscription. The 30-day money-back guarantees most providers offer are real and honored, but it is still cleaner to test on a monthly basis if you are uncertain.

### Paid Add-Ons: Useful vs. Redundant

Most VPN providers now offer optional add-ons. Some are genuinely valuable:

**Worth considering:**
- **NordVPN Threat Protection Pro:** Malware blocking, ad blocking, tracker blocking — essentially a lightweight content filter at the DNS level. Worth it if you don't have a dedicated DNS filter.
- **Surfshark One (antivirus + alert):** Packages antivirus and data breach alerts with the VPN. If you need antivirus anyway, the bundle is cost-effective.

**Often redundant:**
- **Password manager add-ons:** Both NordPass and Surfshark's password manager are decent but not as feature-rich as dedicated options. If you already use 1Password or Bitwarden, skip these.
- **Cloud storage add-ons:** VPN providers are not your best option for cloud storage. Use a dedicated service.

## The Hidden Costs: What VPN Pricing Never Tells You

### Renewal Pricing

This is the most significant hidden cost in the VPN market. Almost every provider offers heavy discounts for new subscribers — often 70-80% off. When your 2-year plan renews, it almost always renews at the standard rate, which is significantly higher.

NordVPN's introductory 2-year plan at $3.69/month renews at approximately $4.99/month for the subsequent 2-year period — still reasonable, but a ~35% price increase. Some providers have larger renewal jumps.

**What to do:** Set a calendar reminder before your plan renews. Contact customer support and ask for a renewal discount — they almost always have promotions available. If the renewal price is too high, cancel and sign up as a "new" customer at a promotional rate (using a different email address if necessary — this is a common and accepted practice).

### Platform Coverage

Make sure the VPN you choose has apps for all your devices. Most major providers cover Windows, Mac, iOS, Android, and Linux. Router support is an additional feature for those who want whole-network VPN coverage — NordVPN, ExpressVPN, and Surfshark all provide router support, though setup complexity varies.

### Streaming Library Access

If you primarily want a VPN to access geo-restricted streaming content, speed and IP address quality matter more than price. NordVPN and ExpressVPN both maintain active unblocking capabilities for major streaming platforms. Some cheaper providers are quickly detected and blocked.
