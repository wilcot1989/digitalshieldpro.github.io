This is the question I get most often: "Should I switch from Gmail to ProtonMail?" After using both as primary email accounts (Gmail 2008-2025, ProtonMail since May 2025), I have an honest answer.

Short version: yes, but only if you understand what you're trading. ProtonMail is better in every way that matters for privacy. Gmail is better in every way that matters for integration with Google's ecosystem. Pick your priority.

---

## Privacy: ProtonMail by a mile

### What Gmail actually does with your data

Google's official statement (since 2017): we don't scan Gmail for ad targeting. True — but there's nuance:

- Gmail content **is** processed for spam detection, smart compose, smart reply, search indexing
- Content **is** used to train Google's machine learning models (anonymized)
- Content **is** subject to subpoena. Google complied with **40,000+ government data requests** in 2024
- Metadata (sender, recipient, subject, time) is retained indefinitely
- IP addresses, devices, locations are logged

The data stays "within Google" — but Google's commercial model and surveillance partnerships mean your email isn't truly private.

### What ProtonMail does

- **Zero-access encryption** at rest. Proton literally cannot read your inbox.
- **End-to-end encryption** between Proton users.
- **Swiss jurisdiction** — Proton complied with 7 court orders in 2024 (vs Google's 40,000+).
- **No metadata retention** beyond what's legally required.
- **No machine learning training** on your emails.

For privacy: ProtonMail wins decisively.

## Features: Gmail by a mile

### Where Gmail dominates

- **Search**: Lightning-fast. Years of email findable in milliseconds.
- **Smart Compose / Smart Reply**: Saves real time daily.
- **Integration**: Calendar, Drive, Docs, Photos, YouTube, Maps, Android — all interconnected.
- **AI features**: Summarize threads, suggest replies, detect important emails — actually useful.
- **Storage**: 15 GB free vs ProtonMail's 1 GB.
- **Business features**: Shared inboxes, delegation, scheduled sending, snooze.
- **Mobile experience**: Most polished email app available, period.

For pure features: Gmail wins decisively.

## Daily-use comparison after 12 months ProtonMail

Things I miss from Gmail:
- Search speed (Proton's encrypted search is good but slower)
- "Schedule send" exists but less prominent in UI
- Smart Compose (no equivalent — not even with Proton's recent AI features)
- Quickly previewing attached PDFs (Proton requires download)
- Filter sophistication (Gmail filters are more flexible)

Things I'm relieved to leave behind:
- Ad-targeted feel (subtle but present in Gmail)
- "Promotions" tab burying legit newsletters
- Knowing my email patterns train Google's models
- Account verification with Google's complex security questions

Net: lost some convenience, gained privacy peace-of-mind. Acceptable trade.

## The migration journey

If you decide to switch, here's a realistic timeline:

**Week 1: Plan**
- Sign up for Proton Free
- Set up Gmail forwarding to Proton address
- Test for 1 week — read your incoming mail in both

**Week 2: Migrate history**
- Use Proton Easy Switch to import Gmail history
- Wait 18-72 hours for full import

**Week 3-8: Update accounts**
- Update email on important sites (banks, taxes, work) to Proton address
- Lower-priority sites can stay on Gmail forwarding

**Months 3-12: Phase out Gmail**
- Don't delete Gmail. Just stop using it as primary.
- Keep forwarding active so legacy contacts still reach you
- Most contacts naturally migrate to your new address over 6-12 months

**Year 2+: Decide on Gmail**
- Most people keep Gmail forever as backup
- Some delete it after 18+ months stable on Proton
- Either way, the active migration is ~3 months

## When you SHOULDN'T switch

❌ **Stay on Gmail if:**
- Your work depends on Google Workspace integration
- You use Google Docs/Sheets daily and need email integration
- You're in countries where ProtonMail is blocked (China, Russia, parts of Middle East)
- You need Gmail's specific business features (shared inboxes, delegation)
- You can't afford €9.99/month for a paid email service (Free is enough for casual)

✅ **Switch to ProtonMail if:**
- You care about email privacy and Google's data practices
- You use email for personal correspondence, not just work
- You want one provider for email + VPN + storage (Unlimited bundle)
- You're tired of Gmail's "Promotions" filter and ad-targeting feel
- You want Swiss jurisdiction over US
