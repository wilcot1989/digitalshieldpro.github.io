Your phone number is one of the most-abused identifiers in 2026. Data brokers cross-reference it with your name, address, family members, and historical addresses. Loyalty programs collect it. Apps demand it for verification. Once it leaks — and it has, repeatedly — it is the seed for spam, scam, and SIM-swap attacks for the rest of your life.

The fix is the same as for email: use a layer of disposable numbers between your real number and the world. Real number for family, doctor, bank. Disposable numbers for everything else.

I have used MySudo, Hushed, Cloaked and Burner over the past year as that layer. Here is what actually works.

---

## What "disposable phone number" means in 2026

These services give you one or more phone numbers that look like real US/Canada/UK mobile numbers (sometimes labelled "VoIP" by carrier-side databases, sometimes not), routed through their app to your real device. SMS and voice arrive in their app, not in your iOS Messages or Android Phone app.

Three flavours:

1. **Identity layer apps** (MySudo, Cloaked) — designed to give you persistent alternative identities, with multiple phone numbers + email + payment alias all bundled.
2. **Burner apps** (Hushed, Burner) — designed for short-term use, get a number for $2 for a week, throw it away.
3. **Voice-mainly** (Skype, Google Voice) — older generation, increasingly poor for privacy, less reliable for verification.

This article focuses on the first two categories.

## Privacy: what the provider sees

You are routing all SMS and calls through these vendors. They see:

- Every number that messages you
- Every number you message
- Voice metadata (caller ID, duration)
- The content of calls and SMS unless E2E (which only applies inside MySudo for Sudo-to-Sudo and inside Cloaked for Cloaked-to-Cloaked)

So the provider is a critical trust point. Read the privacy policies. As of writing:

- **MySudo:** "We do not store the content of your messages or calls" — but operationally, message content traverses their servers. Believe the spirit, not the absolute claim.
- **Hushed:** Similar posture; servers in Canada
- **Cloaked:** Strong E2E claims for Cloaked-to-Cloaked; standard otherwise
- **Burner:** Standard logging; less detailed transparency

For threat models that include "the provider is compelled to log my messages," none of these is sufficient. Use [Signal](/posts/signal-private-messaging-guide-2026/) for sensitive content with people you care about — and use disposable numbers as the *identifier* rather than the *messaging channel* for serious conversations.

## Common mistakes

1. **Using the same disposable number for everything.** That defeats the purpose — you have just rented a second main number. Use multiple Sudos / personas for different identity contexts.
2. **Giving the disposable number to family and the bank.** When you cancel the service, you lose contact with your aunt and your account-recovery flow. Real number for those.
3. **Forgetting to renew.** If your Sudo lapses, the number is recycled and the next person who gets it can intercept your verification SMS. Auto-renew.
4. **Trusting the provider for sensitive conversations.** They see everything. Use [Signal](/posts/best-secure-messaging-apps-2026/) for sensitive content.
5. **Trying to use these for banking 2FA.** Banks block VoIP. Save yourself the troubleshooting and use the real cellular number for banking specifically.
