## Security and Privacy: What Actually Matters

### Encryption
NordVPN uses **AES-256-GCM** encryption via OpenVPN, and **ChaCha20** through NordLynx. Both are military-grade. The practical difference: ChaCha20 is faster on mobile devices and older hardware, which is why NordLynx feels snappier on phones.

### Panama Jurisdiction
NordVPN is incorporated in Panama -- no mandatory data retention laws, outside the 5/9/14 Eyes alliances. Even if a government issued a subpoena, there's literally nothing to hand over.

**Threat Protection Pro** -- This goes beyond VPN. It blocks malicious websites, scans downloads for malware, and strips trackers from web pages. I leave it on permanently, even when the VPN is off. It caught two phishing domains during my testing period that Chrome's built-in protection missed.

**Kill Switch** -- Two levels:
1. **App Kill Switch** -- Closes specific apps if the VPN drops

I use the internet-level kill switch. Non-negotiable for anyone handling sensitive data.

**Double VPN** -- Routes traffic through two servers in different countries. Overkill for most people, but essential for journalists or anyone in high-risk situations.

## Common Mistakes When Choosing a VPN

I've spent 10+ years in cybersecurity, and these are the mistakes I see people make over and over:

### 1. Choosing based on price alone
Surfshark at $2.29/month looks tempting. But when I tested it, speeds dropped 22% compared to NordVPN on the same servers. If you're streaming in 4K or gaming competitively, that difference is noticeable. Cheap VPNs also tend to have fewer servers, leading to congestion during peak hours.

### 2. Ignoring the logging policy
A VPN that logs your activity is worse than no VPN at all -- you're routing all your traffic through a company that records it. Always check if the no-logs policy has been independently audited. "We don't log" on a website means nothing without a third-party audit to back it up.

### 3. Falling for "lifetime" VPN deals
Any VPN offering a "lifetime plan" for $30-50 is a red flag. Running a global server network is expensive. These providers either sell your data, inject ads, or simply shut down. Stick with providers who charge a sustainable monthly fee.

### 4. Not testing the kill switch
A VPN without a kill switch -- or with a kill switch you haven't tested -- leaves gaps. If the VPN connection drops for even a second, your real IP and DNS requests leak. I've tested kill switches from 15 providers. Some take 2-3 seconds to activate. NordVPN's triggers in under 500 milliseconds.

### 5. Assuming all VPN protocols are equal
OpenVPN is solid but slow. IKEv2 is fast but less flexible. WireGuard (and NordVPN's NordLynx variant) offers the best balance of speed and security in 2026. If your VPN doesn't support WireGuard, you're leaving performance on the table.

## Does NordVPN Work in China?

Short answer: sometimes. NordVPN doesn't officially guarantee access from China, but their **obfuscated servers** are specifically designed to bypass deep packet inspection (DPI) used by restrictive networks. During my conversations with users who travel to China regularly, the consensus is:

- Obfuscated servers work most of the time, but not every time
- You need to download and configure NordVPN before entering China
- Having 2-3 backup server locations helps
- Performance is slower than regular connections (expect 30-50 Mbps)

If reliable access from China is your primary use case, NordVPN is among the better options, but no VPN guarantees 100% uptime behind the Great Firewall.
